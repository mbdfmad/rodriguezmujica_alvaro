What is the relationship between height and salary in the nba?¶

It becomes more clear once we group all nba players by their heights. Evem though you wouldn't say there exists a positive linear correlation between height and salary in the NBA, we can see a trend. Taller players seem to get paid more than shorter players, meaning they are more valuable to a team. It seems hard to believe now that the NBA has become a league where its mostly threes, which you would think would benefit the shorter guys since they usually are better three point shooters. I believe if I had used data from 1995, we would have seen a positive relationship between height and salary, since back in the day, centers like Shaq, Olajuwon, Tim Duncan, David Robinson, etc, dominated the league and so were the highest paid players. The relation between weigh and salary, follows the same positive trend as heigh, which makes intuitive sense since taller players are heavier. However, the slope of the linear regression is not as steep. I believe this is because since the game now is not as physical, we are seeing a lot of superstars like Kevin Durant, Kristaps Porzingis, Brandom Ingram, etc, that are very tall but really skinny and have developed their game into perimeter players and have become really good shooters.

Do younger players get paid more money than older players? or does the NBA reward veteran players?¶

We can observe a negative relationship between salary and your birth year. Or in other words, a positive relationship between salary and age. Older players seem to be getting paid significantly more than younger players. We can see how players born from 1997 to 2000, get paid the least amount in general, and its pretty much the same amount. This is because they have rookie contracts. Rookie contracts last 3-4 years, and unless you are a top 5 pick, you never get more than 3-5 milliion a year. We can also observe how players who were born between 1987 and 1992 are getting paid the most money. This is because those players are in their late 20s - early 30s, and those are usually your prime years.

Multiple Regression Model¶

Let's run a multiple regression, in which avg_salary is the dependent variable, and height, weight, and birth_year are the independent variables.

tidy() produces a tibble in which we see relevant information about my model. Something that caught my attention are the p-values. The p-values for height and weight, 0.66 and 0.83 respectively, are pretty high which indicates that my evidence is not strong enough to suggest that an significant effect exists between salary and height and weight. However, the p-value for birth_year is almost 0 which tells us there exists a clear relationship between salary and birth_year.

glance() report information about the entire model. I was able to show how well does the model fit the observed data. It turns out that it fits the model very well. Both the r.squared and the adj.r.squared have a value of >.7 .

augment() adds information about observations to my dataset which includes predicted values in the .fitted column, residuals in the .resid column, and standardized residual values in the .std.resid column. In the .fitted column, we see the values that are calculated after the best model that fits the data. Therefore we would want the .fitted columnn to be as close as the dependent variablee(salary), or in other words, we would want for the values in the .resid column to be as low as possible which would mean that our model is pretty close to the best model that fits the data possible.
